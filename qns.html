Quick Answers About Silk Aura
What is Silk Aura? ▼
Silk Aura is a B2B digital platform dedicated to connecting silk yarn producers, traders, and textile manufacturers across South India. It streamlines the sourcing and trading of high-quality silk products while promoting sustainable practices.
What types of silk does Silk Aura support? ▼
Mulberry Silk – Finest, lustrous silk.
Tasar & Kosa Silk – Coarse and natural gold-toned silk.
Eri Silk – Also known as “peace silk.”
Muga Silk – Golden-yellow silk from Assam.
Bhagalpuri, Kanjeevaram, Banarasi, Dupion – Region-specific luxury silks.
What is the contribution of Karnataka to India's silk production? ▼
Karnataka is the largest producer of silk in India, contributing approximately 9,000 metric tons, which accounts for nearly 45% of the country's total mulberry silk production.
How significant is Andhra Pradesh in India's silk industry? ▼
Andhra Pradesh is the second-largest silk producer in India, with a production of around 6,550 metric tons. The state is renowned for its Pochampally silk, a traditional Ikat style of weaving.
What role does Tamil Nadu play in silk production? ▼
Tamil Nadu ranks fourth in silk production in India, producing approximately 1,185 metric tons. The state is famous for its Kanchipuram silk sarees, known for their rich texture and intricate designs.
What is the overall contribution of South India to India's silk production? ▼
South Indian states—Karnataka, Andhra Pradesh, and Tamil Nadu—collectively contribute over 85% of India's total silk production, making the region the backbone of the country's sericulture industry.
How has India's raw silk production trended in recent years? ▼
India's raw silk production increased from 31,906 metric tons in 2017-18 to 38,913 metric tons in 2023-24. This growth is supported by the expansion of mulberry plantations and enhanced sericulture practices.
What is the export value of India's silk products? ▼
Exports of silk and silk goods from India rose from ₹1,649.48 crores in 2017-18 to ₹2,027.56 crores in 2023-24, indicating a growing global demand for Indian silk.
What is the market size and growth projection for India's sericulture industry? ▼
The Indian sericulture market reached a size of INR 612.7 billion in 2024 and is projected to grow at a CAGR of 14.6%, reaching INR 2,217.5 billion by 2033.

What are ‘Warp’, ‘Weft’, and ‘Ply’ in silk production? ▼
Warp: Vertical yarns giving fabric strength
Weft: Horizontal threads that form the fabric's width/design
Ply: Indicates yarn thickness (e.g., 2-ply = light, 8-ply = heavy)
What is yarn denier and why is it important? ▼
Denier measures the thickness of the silk thread. It affects:
Fabric softness
Durability
Weight and texture
Ranges from 20D (fine) to 200D (thick)
